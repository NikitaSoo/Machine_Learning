# Activity 1: What If Your Group Could Build an LLM?

## Objective

In this group activity, you will imagine that your team has enough time, data, hardware, and funding to build a **custom large language model (LLM)** for a real-world purpose.

The goal is **not** to design the full technical system in detail. The goal is to think about:
- what problem the LLM would solve,
- who would use it,
- why it would be useful,
- what risks or limitations it might have.

## Main Question

Discuss this question in your group:

`If your group had enough resources, what custom LLM would you build?`

## Group Task

Discuss the following points together:

1. **Purpose**
What would your LLM do?

2. **Target users**
Who would use it? For example:
- doctors,
- nurses,
- security analysts,
- teachers,
- software developers,
- IoT engineers,
- emergency response teams.

3. **Importance**
Why would this LLM be useful or valuable?

4. **Practical use**
How would people use it in everyday work?

5. **Personalization**
Would the LLM give different answers depending on the user, task, or context?

6. **Data and knowledge**
What kind of information would the model need to know?

7. **Risks and limitations**
What could go wrong? What should the model **not** be allowed to do?

## Example Idea Areas

Your group may choose any area, but here are some example domains to help you get started.

### Health
- An LLM that helps summarize patient notes for healthcare professionals.
- An LLM that explains medical instructions in simpler language for patients.
- An LLM that helps staff search hospital procedures and guidelines more quickly.

### Security
- An LLM that helps analysts summarize security alerts.
- An LLM that explains suspicious log entries in plain language.
- An LLM that helps incident response teams prepare reports.

### Offensive AI or Offensive Security Research
- An LLM designed for **controlled, legal, and ethical security training environments**.
- An LLM that helps students understand how phishing messages are structured so they can learn to detect them.
- An LLM that helps a red team generate training scenarios for authorized cybersecurity exercises.

Important: if your group chooses this area, frame it as **defensive learning, authorized testing, or research**, not as harmful or illegal use.

### IoT
- An LLM that helps interpret sensor data from smart devices.
- An LLM that explains errors in IoT systems in simple language.
- An LLM that assists technicians in troubleshooting connected devices.

## Output from the Group

Prepare a short summary that you can present when the class returns from the breakout room.

Your group should be ready to explain:

1. the name of your imagined LLM,
2. the field or industry it is designed for,
3. the main problem it solves,
4. the people who would use it,
5. one benefit,
6. one risk or limitation.

## Example Response

Example idea:

- **Name:** SecureLog Assistant
- **Field:** Cybersecurity
- **Purpose:** Helps analysts understand large volumes of system and network logs.
- **Users:** Security analysts and incident response teams
- **Benefit:** Saves time by summarizing suspicious events
- **Risk:** May produce incorrect explanations or miss important context

## Presentation Reminder

Each group will briefly share its idea and key findings with the rest of the class after the breakout-room discussion.
