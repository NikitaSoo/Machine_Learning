# Activity 1 (CyberSim Trainer):

---

## Main Question

---

Discuss this question in your group:

`If your group had enough resources,
what custom LLM would you build?`

## Group task

---

Discuss the following points together:

1. **Purpose** What would your LLM do?

`Answer: Helps security professionals learn and practice
 threat detection, incident response, and attack recognition
 in a safe, authorized training environment.`

2. **Target users** Who would use it? For example:

- doctors,
- nurses,
- teachers,
- software developers,
- IoT engineers,
- emergency response teams.

`Answer: Security analysts, SOC trainees, red/blue team members,
 cybersecurity students and instructors.`

3. **Importance** Why would this LLM be useful or valuable?

`Answer: Cybersecurity talent is in short supply. Traditional training
 is slow and expensive. CyberSim provides realistic,
 on-demand, risk-free practice that speeds up skill development.`

4. **Practical use** How would people use it in everyday work?

`Answer: Explain suspicious alerts or log entries
Generate realistic attack scenarios for team drills
Guide analysts through incident response steps
Help write incident reports`

5. **Personalization** Would the LLM give different answers depending on the user, task, or context?

`Answer: Yes — junior analysts get simple explanations,
 seniors get technical depth, instructors get scenario
 and quiz generation tools.`

6. **Data and knowledge** What kind of information would the model need to know?

`Answer: MITRE ATT&CK, CVE databases, SIEM log samples,
 anonymized incident reports, cybersecurity standards (NIST, ISO 27001)
 and so on...`

7. **Risks and limitations** What could go wrong? What should the model not be allowed to do?

`Answer: Could be misused outside training context
 May generate incorrect technical explanations
 Must never provide working exploits or assist with real attacks`

## Our Example Response

- **Name**: CyberSim Trainer
- **Field**: Cybersecurity
- **Purpose**: Helps to learn and practice threat detection, incident response, and attack recognition
- **Users**: Security analysts, SOC trainees, red/blue team members, cybersecurity students and instructors
- **Benefit**: Will save users a lot of time instead of scrolling internet sources one by one
- **Risk**: May generate incorrect technical explanations and Must never provide working exploits or assist with real attacks
